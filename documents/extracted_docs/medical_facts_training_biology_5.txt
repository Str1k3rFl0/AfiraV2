The potassium channel selectivity filter uses carbonyl oxygen atoms to mimic the hydration shell of K+ ions.
The 'ball-and-chain' model explains the rapid inactivation of voltage-gated sodium channels.
V(D)J recombination uses RAG1 and RAG2 proteins to create diversity in antibodies and T-cell receptors.

Clathrin-coated vesicles are used for endocytosis and transport from the trans-Golgi network.

Rab proteins are small GTPases that ensure vesicles fuse with the correct target membrane.
SNARE proteins (v-SNARE and t-SNARE) provide the mechanical force for membrane fusion.
NSF and alpha-SNAP act to disassemble SNARE complexes after fusion is complete.
The mannose-6-phosphate (M6P) tag targets proteins specifically to the lysosome.
Zellweger syndrome is caused by a failure to import proteins into peroxisomes.
The mitochondrial transit peptide forms an amphipathic alpha-helix for recognition by TOM/TIM complexes.
TOM (Translocase of the Outer Membrane) and TIM (Inner Membrane) complexes facilitate mitochondrial protein import.
HSP70 chaperones keep mitochondrial precursor proteins in an unfolded state during transport.
The electrochemical proton gradient across the inner mitochondrial membrane provides the energy for protein import.
Nuclear Localization Signals (NLS) are typically rich in basic amino acids like lysine and arginine.

The Ran-GTPase cycle provides the directionality for nuclear import and export.
RCC1 is the Ran-GEF (Guanine nucleotide Exchange Factor) located inside the nucleus.
Ran-GAP (GTPase Activating Protein) is located in the cytosol, promoting Ran-GTP hydrolysis.
The nuclear lamina is composed of lamin proteins that provide structural support to the nuclear envelope.
Progeria is caused by mutations in the Lamin A gene, leading to nuclear instability.
The TATA-binding protein (TBP) distorts the DNA double helix to facilitate transcription initiation.
TFIIH possesses both helicase activity for DNA melting and kinase activity to phosphorylate RNA polymerase II.
The C-terminal domain (CTD) of RNA polymerase II acts as a scaffold for mRNA processing factors.

Exon junction complexes (EJCs) remain on the mRNA to signal that splicing is complete.
Nonsense-mediated decay (NMD) degrades mRNAs that contain premature stop codons detected by EJCs.
The polyadenylation signal (AAUAAA) triggers cleavage of the nascent mRNA and addition of the poly-A tail.

Aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases possess a 'proofreading' or editing site to remove incorrectly paired amino acids.
The ribosome's peptidyl transferase activity is located in the 23S/28S rRNA (large subunit).
Translation initiation factor eIF4E binds the mRNA cap and is a major target for regulation.
IRES (Internal Ribosome Entry Sites) allow for cap-independent translation in some viral and cellular mRNAs.

The 'wobble' position (3rd base of codon) allows G to pair with U and Inosine to pair with A, C, or U.
Cyclin D/CDK4/6 phosphorylates the Retinoblastoma (Rb) protein to release the E2F transcription factor.

Cytochrome c release from mitochondria activates Apaf-1 to form the apoptosome.
Caspase-9 is the initiator caspase for the intrinsic pathway.
Caspase-8 is the initiator caspase for the extrinsic pathway (Death Receptor pathway).
Executioner caspases (Caspase-3, 6, 7) cleave cellular substrates like lamin and ICAD.
The Warburg effect refers to cancer cells' preference for glycolysis even in the presence of oxygen.
Telomerase is a reverse transcriptase that carries its own RNA template.
The Hayflick limit is the number of times a normal cell population will divide before stopping.

G-alpha-s activates adenylyl cyclase, whereas G-alpha-i inhibits it.
G-alpha-q activates phospholipase C-beta, leading to IP3 and DAG production.

Pertussis toxin locks G-alpha-i in the 'off' state.

Arrestin binding prevents G-protein interaction and promotes receptor endocytosis.
Ras-GTP activates the Raf kinase (MAPKKK), starting the MAPK cascade.
Scaffold proteins like KSR organize the Raf-MEK-ERK signaling module.
The PH domain of Akt (PKB) binds to PIP3, produced by PI3-kinase.
PTEN is a phosphatase that antagonizes PI3-kinase by dephosphorylating PIP3.
mTOR is a central regulator of cell growth and metabolism, sensitive to nutrient levels.
JAK-STAT signaling is the primary pathway used by cytokines and growth hormone.
Suppressor of Cytokine Signaling (SOCS) proteins provide negative feedback to the JAK-STAT pathway.
The Notch signaling pathway involves proteolytic cleavage of the receptor, moving the intracellular tail to the nucleus.

Wnt signaling stabilizes beta-catenin by inhibiting the destruction complex (APC/Axin/GSK3).
Hedgehog signaling involves the inhibition of Patched, allowing Smoothened to signal.
Autocrine signaling involves a cell responding to its own secreted molecules.
Paracrine signaling acts on nearby cells through local diffusion.
Nitric oxide (NO) signals by activating soluble guanylyl cyclase to produce cGMP.
Nitroglycerin treats angina by acting as a source of NO, causing vasodilation.
Sildenafil (Viagra) inhibits phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE5), maintaining cGMP levels.
Nuclear receptors for thyroid hormone are always bound to DNA, even without ligand.
Co-repressors are displaced by co-activators when a ligand binds to a nuclear receptor.
The glucocorticoid receptor is held in the cytosol by HSP90 until cortisol binds.
Epigenetic marks are maintained through cell division by maintenance methyltransferases (DNMT1).
The 'Histone Code' hypothesis suggests that combinations of modifications dictate gene activity.
H3K4 methylation is generally associated with active promoters.

X-inactivation is mediated by the non-coding RNA Xist, which coats the inactive X chromosome.
Genomic imprinting often involves the IGF2 gene (paternal expression) and H19 gene (maternal expression).

RNA interference (RNAi) can be used to 'knock down' mRNA levels in laboratory research.
The RISC complex (RNA-Induced Silencing Complex) uses siRNA to find and cleave matching mRNA.
Dicer is the ribonuclease that processes long dsRNA into siRNAs.
Long non-coding RNAs (lncRNAs) can act as 'decoys' for miRNAs or scaffolds for chromatin modifiers.
Circular RNAs (circRNAs) are highly stable and can act as 'sponges' for miRNAs.

The Major Histocompatibility Complex (MHC) is the most polymorphic region of the human genome.

The epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT) is crucial for development and cancer metastasis.

The 'sorting out' of cells in an aggregate is driven by differences in the type and level of cadherin expression.

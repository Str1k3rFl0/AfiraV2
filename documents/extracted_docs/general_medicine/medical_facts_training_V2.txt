Differential Medical Facts for AI Training
Tachycardia is a fast heart rate (>100 bpm), whereas Bradycardia is a
slow heart rate (<60 bpm).
Hypertension refers to high blood pressure, while Hypotension refers to
low blood pressure.
Hyperglycemia is high blood sugar, whereas Hypoglycemia is dangerously
low blood sugar.
The Afferent neurons carry signals toward the brain, while Efferent
neurons carry signals away from the brain.
The Arteries carry oxygenated blood away from the heart, except for the
pulmonary artery.
The Veins carry deoxygenated blood toward the heart, except for the
pulmonary vein.
Ligaments connect bone to bone, while Tendons connect muscle to bone.
Dysphagia is difficulty swallowing, whereas Dysphasia is a language
disorder (speech).
Apnea is the temporary cessation of breathing, while Dyspnea is
difficult or labored breathing.
Hematuria is blood in the urine, while Hemoptysis is coughing up blood
from the lungs.
Anemia is a lack of red blood cells, while Leukemia is a cancer of the
white blood cells.
The Axial skeleton is the central axis (skull, spine), while the
Appendicular skeleton is the limbs and girdles.
Abduction is moving a limb away from the midline, while Adduction is
moving it toward the midline.
The Sympathetic nervous system triggers 'fight or flight', while the
Parasympathetic triggers 'rest and digest'.
Systolic pressure is the pressure during heart contraction, while
Diastolic is the pressure during heart relaxation.
Endocrine glands secrete hormones into the blood, while Exocrine glands
secrete through ducts (like sweat).
A Thrombus is a blood clot that stays in place, while an Embolus is a
clot that travels through the bloodstream.
Prone position is lying face down, while Supine position is lying face
up.
Mastication is the act of chewing, while Deglutition is the act of
swallowing.
Osteoblasts are cells that build bone, while Osteoclasts are cells that
break down bone tissue.
Acute conditions start suddenly and are short-term, while Chronic
conditions develop slowly and persist long-term.
Antigens are foreign substances that trigger immune responses, while
Antibodies are proteins produced to fight them.
Hypoxia is low oxygen in tissues, while Hypoxemia is low oxygen
specifically in the blood.
Infection is the invasion of pathogens, while Inflammation is the
body's response to injury or infection.
Benign tumors do not spread, while Malignant tumors are cancerous and
can invade other tissues.
Proximal means closer to the trunk of the body, while Distal means
further away from the trunk.
Superior means toward the head, while Inferior means toward the feet.
The Epidermis is the top layer of skin, while the Dermis is the thick
layer underneath it.
Type 1 Diabetes is an insulin deficiency, while Type 2 Diabetes is
insulin resistance.
Ureters carry urine from kidneys to the bladder, while the Urethra
carries urine out of the body.
The Cerebrum controls high-level functions, while the Cerebellum
controls balance and coordination.
White matter consists of myelinated axons, while Gray matter consists
mainly of neuronal cell bodies.
Ischemia is a restriction in blood supply, while Infarction is tissue
death due to lack of blood supply.
A Sign is an objective finding seen by a doctor, while a Symptom is a
subjective feeling reported by a patient.
Vasoconstriction is the narrowing of blood vessels, while Vasodilation
is the widening of blood vessels.
Atrophy is the wasting away of tissue, while Hypertrophy is the
increase in volume of an organ or tissue.
Stenosis is the abnormal narrowing of a body channel, while Dilation is
the widening.
The Right Lung has 3 lobes, while the Left Lung has 2 lobes to
accommodate the heart.
Visceral pleura covers the lungs, while Parietal pleura lines the chest
cavity.
Aerobic exercise requires oxygen, while Anaerobic exercise occurs
without sufficient oxygen.
Auscultation is listening to body sounds, while Palpation is feeling
the body with hands.
The Radius is on the thumb side of the forearm, while the Ulna is on
the pinky side.
The Tibia is the shin bone (inner), while the Fibula is the smaller
outer leg bone.
Etiology is the cause of a disease, while Pathogenesis is the manner of
its development.
Prognosis is the predicted outcome of a disease, while Diagnosis is the
identification of the disease.
Somatic nervous system is voluntary, while Autonomic nervous system is
involuntary.
Bile is produced by the Liver and stored in the Gallbladder.
The Small Intestine absorbs nutrients, while the Large Intestine
absorbs water and forms stool.
Myopia is nearsightedness, while Hyperopia is farsightedness.
Enzyme is a biological catalyst, while a Hormone is a chemical
messenger.

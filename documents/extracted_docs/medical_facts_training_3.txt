The human skeleton is divided into the axial skeleton and the
appendicular skeleton.
The cranium consists of eight cranial bones that protect the brain.
The vertebral column is composed of 33 vertebrae in infants and 26 in
adults.
The hyoid bone is the only bone in the body that does not articulate
with any other bone.
The humerus is the long bone in the upper arm that connects the
shoulder to the elbow.
The radius and ulna are the two long bones located in the forearm.
The carpal bones consist of eight small bones that make up the wrist.
The pelvis is formed by the hip bones, sacrum, and coccyx.
The tibia is the larger and stronger of the two bones in the lower leg.
The fibula is the slender bone located on the lateral side of the
tibia.
The patella, also known as the kneecap, is the largest sesamoid bone in
the body.
The phalanges are the bones that form the fingers and toes.
The myocardium is the muscular middle layer of the wall of the heart.
The pericardium is a double-walled sac that encloses the heart and the
roots of the great vessels.
The endocardium is the innermost layer of tissue that lines the
chambers of the heart.
The tricuspid valve is located between the right atrium and the right
ventricle.
The mitral valve, or bicuspid valve, is located between the left atrium
and the left ventricle.
The septum is the wall of tissue that separates the left and right
sides of the heart.

The trachea, or windpipe, is a cartilaginous tube that connects the
larynx to the bronchi.
The pleura is a serous membrane that folds back onto itself to form a
two-layered membranous pleural sac.
The lobes of the right lung are three, while the left lung has only two
lobes.
The esophagus is a muscular tube through which food passes from the
pharynx to the stomach.
The pyloric sphincter is a band of smooth muscle at the junction
between the pylorus of the stomach and the duodenum.
The duodenum is the first and shortest segment of the small intestine.
The jejunum is the middle segment of the small intestine, located
between the duodenum and the ileum.
The ileum is the final section of the small intestine.
The cecum is the beginning of the large intestine.
The ascending, transverse, descending, and sigmoid parts comprise the
colon.
The nephron is the microscopic structural and functional unit of the
kidney.
The ureters are tubes made of smooth muscle fibers that propel urine
from the kidneys to the urinary bladder.
The cerebral hemispheres are connected by a large bundle of nerve
fibers called the corpus callosum.
The cerebellum is located under the occipital lobes of the cerebral
hemispheres.
The brainstem consists of the midbrain, pons, and medulla oblongata.
The thalamus acts as a relay station for sensory information traveling
to the cerebral cortex.
The hypothalamus links the nervous system to the endocrine system via
the pituitary gland.
The meninges are the three membranes that envelop the brain and spinal
cord.
The dura mater is the outermost and toughest of the three meninges.
The arachnoid mater is the middle layer of the meninges, named for its
spider-web-like appearance.
The pia mater is the delicate innermost layer of the meninges.
The sciatic nerve is the largest and longest nerve in the human body.
The epidermis is the outermost layer of the skin, providing a
waterproof barrier.
The dermis is the layer of skin beneath the epidermis that contains
connective tissue and hair follicles.
The hypodermis is the deepest layer of the skin, consisting mostly of
adipose tissue.
The endocrine system consists of glands that secrete hormones directly
into the circulatory system.
The adrenal glands are located on top of each kidney.
The spleen is the largest organ in the lymphatic system.
The gallbladder is a small hollow organ where bile is stored and
concentrated.
The pharynx is part of both the digestive and respiratory systems.
The larynx, or voice box, contains the vocal cords.
The periosteum is a membrane that covers the outer surface of all
bones.
